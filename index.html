Hello, I'm Carlo!

I am a web developer that just made a career transition from structural engineering. In my last position, I designed bridges—primarily in New England, but some across the United States. Many of my most exciting bridge design experiences involved creating models using proprietary software or visual basic to optimize the designs. These experiences reminded me of my experience in graduate school, where I used software to model the behavior of buildings during earthquakes. As I considered my career change, I reflected on my enjoyment solving problems using code.

At the same time, I have been a lifelong leader in athletic endeavors, mostly as a captain of various swim teams from childhood through the collegiate level, then as the Athletic Director of Boston Triathlon Team today. One of my greatest athletic passions has always been understanding training/performance data, and how it translates to athletic improvements. I have always enjoyed “looking at the numbers” with respect to sports that I participate in. The combination of my passions for solving problems with code and analysis during my athletic endeavors makes the role of a software developer in a team environment a natural fit for me.
